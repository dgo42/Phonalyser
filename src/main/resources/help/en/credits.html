Credits & acknowledgements

Inspiration

Inspired by the spirit of REW (Room EQ Wizard) and similar audio-measurement software. Independently designed and implemented.

With thanks

First and foremost, to Nick Sukhov — building his power-amplifier and phono-preamp designs, and wanting to measure them properly, is where this whole project began. Phonalyser exists because of that spark.

A renowned audio engineer. In 1988–89 he designed the celebrated high-fidelity power amplifier known as the ВВ (vysokaya vernost — "high fidelity") and a phono preamplifier that already implemented the IEC amendment in 1989. He went on to develop the dynamic-biasing system for cassette decks at the Kyiv company Mayak, and served as editor-in-chief of Radio-Hobby magazine. He is still designing today: his latest high-fidelity power amplifier reaches one of the lowest distortion figures ever published — around 0.00008 % THD at 100 W. Measuring a distortion that low — below what typical analysers and soundcards resolve — is exactly the challenge Phonalyser was built to meet.

  • Patreon — Nick Sukhov
  • YouTube — @nick2bike

To Hans Rosenberg, whose video on the noise floor and a tone's fundamental skirt was the nudge that turned a long-running frustration with existing tools into the decision to build a dedicated one. Phonalyser tackles the skirt live, in the running spectrum, rather than as an offline post-processing step.

Measurement hardware

A measurement is only as clean as the converters in front of the software. Phonalyser was developed and validated against reference hardware from these builders:

  • JLSounds — I2SoverUSB, a low-jitter USB-to-I²S / DSD interface that feeds an external DAC.
  • Kaamos Tech — W-DAC v2 / W-DAC XLR, compact high-performance DACs (116 dB SINAD typical).
  • E1DA (Ivan Khlyupin) — Cosmos ADC / ADCiso, an ultra-low-distortion ADC that brings audio-analyser-grade THD+N measurement within reach of the bench.
